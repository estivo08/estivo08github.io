Mac Auto-Typer Guide

Follow these steps to create a simple Mac app that simulates human typing in any application.

Step 1: Copy the AppleScript Code

AppleScript Code 
delay 3

-- Paste your multi-line text between the quotes below:
set myText to "--> REPLACE THIS WITH YOUR TEXT <--"

-- This loop types out the text
tell application "System Events"
	repeat with c in characters of myText
		keystroke c
		delay 0.01
	end repeat
end tell

Step 2: Turn the Code into an App

  1. Open the "Script Editor" app on your Mac.
  2. Paste the code you just copied into the blank white window.
  3. Click File > Save...
  4. Give your app a name (e.g., "Auto-Typer").
  5. Important: Change the `File Format` to `Application`.
  6. Save it somewhere easy to find, like your Desktop.

Step 3: Give Your App Permission

  1. Open System Settings.
  2. Go to Privacy & Security > Accessibility.
  3. Click the `+` (plus) button (enter your password).
  4. Find the "Auto-Typer" app you just saved and click Open.
  5. Make sure the switch next to your app is ON.

How to Use Your New App

  1. Open any text field (like a Google Doc).
  2. Click your mouse cursor where you want the typing to start.
  3. Double-click your "Auto-Typer" app.
  4. After a 3-second delay, it will start typing!
